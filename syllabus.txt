AI-Style Writing Flags

Flag formulaic or pattern-generated prose that resembles large language model output. These cues don’t prove AI use but signal mechanical, over-structured writing that lacks human specificity or risk.

Antithetical phrasing: “Not only …but also,” “If not X, then Y,” “It’s not about X; it’s about Y.”
→ Comment: “This reads like an automated contrast — simplify the claim and drop the built-in opposition.”

Negative parallelism: Several “not …” clauses followed by a single positive pivot (“not A, not B — just C”).
→ Comment: “Too formulaic — rewrite without stacked negatives.”

Over-balanced transitions: Sentences repeatedly joined by “however,” “while,” “although,” or “therefore.”
→ Comment: “Vary transitions — this pattern sounds machine-generated.”

Perfect syntax but empty tone: Flawless grammar, balanced clauses, no idiomatic voice or stakes.
→ Comment: “Reads mechanically precise — add your own voice and risk.”

Statistical diction drift: Overuse of generic academic fillers (“significant,” “complex interplay,” “broader implications,” “critical aspect”).
→ Comment: “Cut vague filler — name concrete people, texts, or stakes.”

Outline cadence: Sentences mirror each other in rhythm or clause shape, creating a rehearsed flow.
→ Comment: “Break the rhythm — rewrite one sentence to sound more natural.”
